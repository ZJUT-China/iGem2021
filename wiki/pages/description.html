Description

ZJUT-CHINA aims to develop a cell-free biosensor as a new application for detecting mRNA associated with disease. The DESCRIPTION page mainly describes our motivation, the description of the whole project and the design of the biosensor.

Insprition

Biomarkers, or biological markers, are measurable indicators for certain diseases. By literature reviewing, we learned that the prognosis of some diseases such as specific cancers, infections resulting from inflammation are closely related with RNA biomarkers. These findings significantly evoke our interests.

Up to date, quantitative real-time PCR is one of the most widely used and well-established methods for RNA assay. Detection kits developed based on this method generally comprise of at least four units (RNA extraction, reverse transcription, amplification and signal output) and time-consuming (2-4 hours). For the development of detection kits for different diseases, design of primers and fluorescent probes for specific nucleic acid sequences are required. Due to its tedious, time-consuming and lack of universality, this technology could not meet the requirements for the rapid and accurate assay of disease during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Different from the requirement for rapid and accurate temperature changes of PCR , the isothermal amplification can be carried out at one temperature. This technology reduces the requirements of instruments for the detection, thus substantially increases its applicability. For example, project Rapidemic (Leiden University, 2020) provided a relatively rapid assay which consists three units: signal amplification with RPA, reporter generation with linear strand-displacement amplification (LSDA), output of visualized signal with GQ-catalyzed oxidation.

Moreover, combination of isothermal amplification with the CRISPR system will further reduce the assay time to less than 1 hour and further improve improve the sensitivity and accuracy of the amplification. For example, Project casx (MOSCOW, 2020) combined RPA with casx proteins. The results can be visualized with strips. However, the process is still quite complex.

Currently, low-cost, simple and universal assays for RNA are badly needed.

Description

In this year, ZJUT_China team is aiming to develop a new method to solve the above problems. Through preliminary investigations and communication with doctors in related fields, we obtained some principles like "from invasive to non-invasive, from tedious to simple". Therefore, we used blood or urine as testing samples to establish a biosensor for rapid detection of RNA. Additionally，according to the literature, most RNA biomarkers are secreted in vitro as exosomes [1], so we wanted to build a biosensor for rapid detection of RNA using a cell-free system[2].

Cell-Free System

Cell-free system based on extracts allow rapid protein expression. Due to its open reaction conditions, it provides a high degree of versatility and flexibility, while bypassing time-consuming processes such as bacterial transformation, clone selection and cell lysis. Simultaneously, it can be more precise to control the reaction substrate and reduce the influence of bacteria on protein production. Their extremely user-friendly and safe processing methods make them equally suitable for cutting-edge research and student education in academia and biotechnology industry.

The cell-free system is composed of three parts: the cell extract, a reaction mixture, and DNA templates.[3]

The cell extract contains ribosomes, RNA polymerase, and other transcription and translation accessory proteins.

Reaction mixture of supplemented cofactors for protein synthesis includes amino acids, nucleotides, salts, polyamines, an energy source, molecular crowding agents, metabolic cofactors, buffer, transfer RNAs, and other metabolic additives.

DNA template supplied to the reaction can be plasmid DNA or a linear expression template obtained from PCR.

In short, the choice of a cell-free system provides a suitable detection environment for our sensors, enabling rapid detection.

CFS entirely relies on the endogenous transcription-translation(TXTL) machinery of E. coli employing the core RNA polymerase and E. coli transcription factor sigma 70 (σ70). Therefore, we chose a promoter that is activated by transcriptional factor σ70 called "P70a" as the starting point in our gene circuits.

Finally, we designed an incoherent feed-forward loop circuit[4], which expresses the deGFP only when the CRISPR/Cas9 system combined with the target RNA.

gRNA

Based on this, we propose a method to detect different RNAs efficiently and cheaply by using CRISPR/Cas9 System in combination with the Cell-free System. In the CRISPR/CAS9 system, gRNA is made up of two parts: crRNA and tracrRNA. Through base pairing, crRNA binds to tracrRNA to form tracrRNA/crRNA complex, which can direct Cas9 protein to cut dsDNA at the sequence target site.

In order to facilitate gene editing and related operations, crRNA and tracrRNA were connected and modified into single-guide RNA (sgRNA) to help Cas9 protein to cut DNA in a directional way[5]. And on that basis,we're going to re-engineer sgRNA to be igRNA that can be responded to by target RNA, the concrete structure is shown in the design part (design a click here and jump to design).

In general, the crRNA of igRNA keeps the loop structure, and the spacer sequence can not bind to the target and thus does not have the function of guiding Cas9 protein cleavage. When igRNA is recognized by target RNA, the loop structure of igRNA opens and spacer sequence is released, the spacer sequence can be combined with the target to guide the directional cutting of CAS9 protein[6].

New application

By introducing the combined igRNA and cas9 into the cell-free system, the RNA in body fluids can be rapidly and accurately detected. Due to its easy operation (as simple as the one-pot method), low cost (less than $20 per test), extreme low RNA leaking risk, and readily design of igRNA for specific target RNA,this program provides a novel application which meets the urgent needs for non-invasive assays of current medical services. Intensive efforts have been made in the research diseases with RNA as Biomarker. Based on the above, this program aims to design specific igRNAs for various Biomarker with different methods and establish a database. With the database, auxiliary indexes for early detection or prognosis of numerous diseases can be readily provided by simply replacing the igRNAs.

Design
Gene circuit

Our gene circuit is mainly composed of three parts: p70a- σ28, p28a-tetR-ssrA and p28a-tetO-degfp. At the same time, we also made a special design for sgRNA, which is called igRNA.

Parts

P70a-σ28: Regulated by the transcriptional factor σ70, P70a is regarded as a promoter which induces the expression of the transcriptional factor σ28.

P28a-tetR-ssrA: The promoter P28a is activated to express the inhibitory protein tetR-ssrA under the regulation of the transcriptional factor σ28.

P28a-tetO-degfp: Under the regulation of the transcriptional factor o28, the promoter P28a can induce the expression of deGFP, but once the tetO binding site binds to the tetR repressor protein, it will inhibit the expression of deGFP.

Circuit principle

We designed an incoherent feedforward circuit [4] in which the transcriptional factor σ70 regulates the expression of σ28 in a cell-free system, and the activated promoter P28a represses tetR-ssrA under the regulation of the transcriptional factor σ28, which binds to the tetO site in the downstream gene circuit to inhibit tetO expression. Therefore, the expression of deGFP gene was inhibited.

In addition, to detect RNA biomarkers, we incorporated the CRISPR/Cas9 system.Cas9 protein can combined with our designed igRNA to form igRNA-Cas9 comple: When the RNA biomarker to be detected is present, igRNA-Cas9 is capable of performing the cleavage function, cutting the target gene tetR-ssrA. Simultaneously, proteins with ssrA degradation tags are degraded at a certain rate in the Cell-free system. In this way, tetR-ssrA inhibition of tetO gene was relieved, deGFP gene could be expressed normally, and the positive test result was characterized.

igRNA

"igRNA" refers to the guide RNA that interacts with each other. When igRNA interacts with a particular small sequence of RNA or long RNA, it releases the original gRNA recognition sequence that binds to the Cas protein[7]. The structure of igRNA is shown in the figure.

IgRNA can be divided into four parts: recognition sequence A, cis-compressed sequence, recognition sequence B and spacer sequence.

Recognition sequence A and recognition sequence B are used to recognize Target RNA, and their sequences are complementary to front part of Target RNA, while cis-compressed sequence is complementary to a part of spacer sequence. Because of this design, it can maintain the structure of its loop structure without traget RNA and can not bind to Cas9 protein. When tragetRNA appears, the structure of the loop is destroyed, the spacer sequence is opened to bind to Cas9.

When the two combine, RNP then has a specific cutting function. The released spacer sequence can be complementary to one of the dsDNA strands which is to be cut and perform the cutting function.
